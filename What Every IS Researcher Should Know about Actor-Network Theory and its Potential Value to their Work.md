The first IS scholars to begin applying ANT were from Europe, including Geoff Walsham from Cambridge University [ 49] [ 50 ], plus scholars from Norway – Eric Monteiro and Ole Hanseth [ 31 ] [ 73 ] –and Sweden (Bo Dahlbom) [ 8]. Largely due to their efforts, ANT slowly grew as a new theoretical perspective represented in the IS discipline. Although Geoff Walsham is often regarded as the major proponent who introduced ANT to the IS community, neither of his works cited above represent empirical studies using ANT. One essay was the argument for why interpretive research was gaining greater recognition in IS research, incorporating the language of ANT to explain this “emergence of interpretive research” [ 49 ]; the other conference paper was in the form of a tutorial presented at IFIP 8.2 [50 ] to introduce IS scholars to the unique terminology in ANT and  the types of scenarios where the approach may be useful. In both of  his conceptual pieces, Walsham introduced the components of ANT theory (e.g., enrollment, translation, interessement) and showed how to incorporate them in studying a longitudinal initiative – which could be a large technology infrastructure project [ 18 ] or the process by which [[interpretive research]] was becoming accepted in the IS community in the 1990s as an alternative to [[positivism]] [ 49 ].![[Screenshot 2026-06-23 at 8.46.04 PM.png]]
